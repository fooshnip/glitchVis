Glitch

Analyzing the MMO's final year of activity


From the dataset posted through Do Everything Forever!, this page will attempt to illustrate the activity in Tiny Speck's browser-based MMORPG Glitch through the final 12 months of operation. We present various visual tactics utilizing d3.js to quickly provide insights to decision makers and developers on analyzing auction house, social network, and forum activity with dashboard-friendly designs. Notably, we hope to provide suggestions for additional ways in which analytics can be applied and presented to help monitor the health of your game...







Player Segmentation Over Time


Our first visual attempts to track movement in monthly player segmentation. Can we identify signs of trouble from shifts in player clusters? While we can utilize clustering methods at each interval to identify sizes of relative groupings, can further value be gained from showing migratory patterns over time? Presented below, a sankey diagram can be an effective way to show flows between intervals.

We used d3 extension sankey.js to show flows here. Mouse over the graph or select a node to drill down on a player segment.

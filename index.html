Glitch

Analyzing the MMO's final year of activity


From the dataset posted through Do Everything Forever!, this page will attempt to illustrate the activity in Tiny Speck's browser-based MMORPG Glitch through the final 14 months of operation. We present various visual tactics utilizing d3.js to quickly provide insights to decision makers and developers on analyzing player behaviors over time. Notably, we hope to provide suggestions for additional ways in which analytics can be applied and presented to help monitor the health of your game...







Visualizating Player Migration Over Time


This first visual tracks movement in monthly player segmentation. Here we catch watch how players diverge and converge over time. We used clustering methods at each monthly interval to identify key groupings across four economic KPIs.

Total Auctions

Average Auctions Posted per Day

Percentage of Auctions that Resulted in Sales

Number of Distinct Items Categories Posted

Marketplace Forum Posting (boolean)


Three overall hierarchies of player types emerged: Casual, Moderate, and Hardcore. Each have subtypes within.

Casual: These players perform below average across all economic indicators. On average, they post between 2-4 auctions per month across 1-2 item categories. Moderate: These players are midrange performers across all dimensions. On average, they post between 11-28 auctions per month across 2-5 item categories. Hardcore: These are the most active users across all KPIs. They typically post 152 auctions per month across 8 item categories. Forum: The primary characterization of this group is their activity in the market place forum. Forum players have a vey similar profile to hardcore players.
